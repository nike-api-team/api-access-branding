The Key Finding

The presenting symptoms — bilateral quadriceps heaviness, progressive power loss, and post-race neurocognitive impairment — are consistent with an O₂ delivery ceiling: cardiac output of fully oxygenated blood at maximum, compounded by EIAH, producing both local (muscular) and systemic (cerebral) effects at race-relevant intensities.

Post-visit spirometry at the University of Newcastle confirmed supernormal lung function — FEV1 129% predicted, FVC 118%, MVV 147 L/min, and supernormal respiratory muscle strength. The lungs are not the limiter. EIAH (SpO₂ 92% at exhaustion) still occurs despite robust lungs — a transit-time phenomenon at high cardiac output that further constrains O₂ delivery.

At sustained race-pace intensities, cardiac output is the primary ceiling. LT2 at 94% HRmax leaves minimal cardiac reserve above threshold. During mile repeats, HR reached 99% of maximum. The system has more extraction capacity than the heart can feed.

Muscle oxidative capacity is robust, and O₂-carrying capacity is not a bottleneck — however, O₂-carrying capacity could be augmented via suggested strategies to overcome other limitations. A right-side motor coordination deficit was identified — a coordination, not strength, asymmetry corroborated by bilateral NIRS and preliminary gait analysis.

Priority interventions: Aerobic capacity development to raise LT1/LT2, stroke volume development via aerobic volume and heat acclimation (5+ weeks), warm-up priming (4–5 min at LT2/LTP), reintroduction of single-leg coordinative S&C, respiratory muscle training, operating lung volume assessment, FiO₂ time trial, and SpO₂ monitoring at altitude.
