From Symptom to System: A Multi-Domain Physiological Investigation in an Elite Middle-Distance Runner

A 14-Day Applied Sport Science Investigation
Nike Sport Research Lab — Athlete Performance Innovation • In collaboration with Oregon Performance Research Lab & University of Newcastle • Beaverton, OR • February–March 2026

Presenting Concern

An elite female 1500m/mile runner reported bilateral quadriceps heaviness, progressive power loss, and post-race lightheadedness/dissociation during major championship finals. Symptoms were absent in training, where true race-length maximal efforts had not been replicated. The investigation sought to determine whether a discrete physiological limitation could be identified across the O₂ transport cascade.

Methods

14-day embedded training camp. 8 sessions monitored spanning easy–maximal intensity. Bilateral NIRS (SmO₂, tHb), HR, GPS, lactate profiling, CO-rebreathing (Hb mass), graded exercise test + TTE, force plate assessment, and gait analysis.

Primary Findings

  • Cardiac output of fully oxygenated blood is the primary ceiling at sustained race-relevant intensities. LT2 at 94% HRmax leaves minimal cardiac reserve above threshold.
  • Lung function is supernormal (FEV1 129%, FVC 118%, MVV 147 L/min, MIPs supernormal) — confirmed post-visit via Newcastle collaboration. The lungs are not the limiter.
  • EIAH still compounds O₂ delivery. SpO₂ dropped to 92% during TTE (−6 pts) despite robust lungs — a transit-time phenomenon at high cardiac output.
  • Muscle oxidative capacity is robust. SmO₂ holds steady at threshold; extraction reaches 5–13% at max.
  • Right-side motor coordination deficit identified on force plate — a coordination, not strength, asymmetry that worsens under cueing demands. Corroborated by bilateral NIRS asymmetries.

Priority Interventions

  • Aerobic capacity development: Raise LT1/LT2 as a fraction of HRmax to expand the sustainable intensity range.
  • Stroke volume development: Aerobic volume and sustained moderate-intensity work to drive eccentric cardiac hypertrophy.
  • Heat & altitude acclimation: Heat (5+ weeks) for plasma volume expansion; altitude for Hb mass to offset EIAH.
  • Warm-up priming: 4–5 min bout near LT2/LTP pace to prime VO₂ kinetics and increase critical speed before racing.
  • Single-leg coordinative S&C: Reintroduce complex single-leg exercises targeting the right-side coordination deficit.
  • Call room management: Strategies to maintain physiological readiness during ~20 min enforced inactivity before race start.
  • Breath-hold priming: Investigate breath-hold protocols as a low-logistics pre-race tool to augment O₂ carrying capacity through the call room window.
