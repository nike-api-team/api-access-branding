The Athlete

A hybrid competitor at the top of his game

Dylan Scott is one of the best HYROX athletes in the world — 3rd place at the 2025 World Championships with a personal best of 54:47. He came to Nike World Headquarters to spend a day with the Applied Performance Innovation team in the Nike Sport Research Lab, learning more about himself and his body during HYROX-style exercise.

The primary goal: review his priming protocol, test race pace, examine thresholds of tolerance, and deploy strategies to improve performance.

The Sport

8 runs. 8 stations. No hiding.

HYROX is a fitness race that alternates 1 km runs with 8 functional workout stations — demanding power, endurance, and durability from every energy system in the body. The elite men's division sees athletes completing this gauntlet in under 60 minutes.

The Session

Simulating the back half of HYROX

To model the demands of the second half of competition — where races are won and lost — Dylan performed a structured warm-up followed by the final five station-and-run sequences: Burpees, Row, Farmers Carry, Lunges, and Wall Balls, each separated by 1 km runs on the indoor track.

Throughout the session, the team captured venous blood lactate, heart rate, and local muscle oxygenation (Moxy sensors on the shoulder, forearm, and quadriceps) to map the shifting physiological demands in real time.

The highest lactate spike — 7.1 mmol/L during Wall Balls — came at the very end of the session. This matches the race structure: the final station demands sustained upper-body work while the entire system is already fatigued from the preceding 45+ minutes.

The Signal

The rotating limiter

Quad oxygen collapsed during station work
Quad Moxy started around 41 at baseline, surged to 95+ from the warm-up priming protocol, then dropped dramatically during high-load stations — bottoming out at ~4% after Lunges and Wall Balls. The quads desaturated almost completely during stations and only partially recovered on each run.
Arm oxygen was remarkably stable — until it wasn't
While quad oxygen swung wildly through the session, arm Moxy remained steady around 32-34 for most of the effort — dropping to a low of 23% during Burpees. The practical takeaway: performance was constrained more by local leg oxygen stress and systemic metabolite load than by broad upper-body collapse.
The runs don't recover you — they maintain you
Lactate never dropped below the previous station's value during a run segment. Running between stations is not recovery — it's managed accumulation. The ability to sustain pace while clearing just enough metabolite is the competitive differentiator.
"The demands for this sport appear to rely heavily on work at upper threshold for a long period of time. That is very difficult."

The Product

Fast shoe first, functional shoe second

Dylan's message was consistent: the elite HYROX athlete wants a running shoe that can handle the stations — not a cross-trainer that happens to run. Speed on the 1 km segments is where the biggest time gains live, and compromising that for station stability is a trade-off most top competitors wouldn't make.

The Path Forward

From lab readout to race execution

Wrapping up the day was a discussion around future opportunities — how to prime, how to recover, and how to train the transitions that separate podium finishers from the rest.

01
Extend the warm-up — aerobic and anaerobic
Priming before a HYROX race should include both aerobic and anaerobic stimulation. Get the system fully primed, not just moving.
02
Practice pacing in training
Train with pacing strategies in workouts to find the just-right sustainable pace. The cost of going too hard early compounds through every remaining station.
03
Post-exercise nutrition
Immediate intake of carbohydrate and protein post-session, and prioritizing sleep or "synthetic sleep" recovery tools like Shiftwave.
04
Train the transitions, not just the pieces
Build sessions that force repeated run-to-station-to-run switches. The ability to manage oxygen recovery while clearing lactate between tasks is where races are won.
